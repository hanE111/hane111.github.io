About Me

I graduated from Seoul National University with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering. I currently work as a research assistant in the Bio and Health Informatics Lab under Professor Sun Kim at SNU. My research interests include AI-driven drug discovery, precision medicine, and using interpretable AI to uncover disease mechanisms.

What drew me to this field was a simple realization: while individual clinical care is invaluable, computational approaches offer unique leverage to reach millions of patients whose diseases are currently treated through trial-and-error. I'm driven by the goal of creating research that translates into real clinical impact.

I believe the human body is one of our greatest sources of inspiration for engineering innovation. By studying biological systems—how cells communicate, how molecular interactions lead to disease—we not only learn to develop better treatments but also discover principles that can advance technology more broadly.

Outside the lab, I stay active through taekwondo, soccer, and snowboarding. I also enjoy classical music and impressionist art—pursuits that remind me there's beauty in both precision and interpretation.

My goal is straightforward: to build an academic career as a researcher whose work reaches millions and saves lives.
