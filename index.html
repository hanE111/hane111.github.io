About Me

I am a research assistant at the Bio&Health Informatics Lab in Seoul National University with a passion for applying artificial intelligence to solve critical problems in drug discovery and precision medicine. Currently, I work as a Research Assistant in Professor Sun Kim's Bio & Health Informatics Lab, where I focus on developing AI models for cancer and neurodegenerative disease drug discovery.

My research spans the intersection of machine learning, computational biology, and clinical applications. I'm particularly interested in how we can leverage multi-modal data and transfer learning to predict patient-specific drug responses and accelerate the drug development pipeline.

Research Interests

AI for Drug Discovery

Developing machine learning models to predict drug-target interactions and patient-specific drug responses for cancer and neurodegenerative diseases.

Transfer Learning in Biomedicine

Investigating knowledge transfer between different biological systems (cell lines, patient tumors, PDX models) to improve model generalization.

Molecular Representations

Integrating 2D and 3D molecular representations with modern language models for improved molecular property prediction and generation.

Precision Medicine

Building computational frameworks to enable personalized treatment strategies based on multi-omics patient data.

Molecular Captioning with GraphT5

Developed a novel approach for generating natural language descriptions of molecular structures by integrating graph neural networks with T5 language models, enabling better interpretability of molecular properties.
